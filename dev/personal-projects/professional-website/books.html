Current Reads

Effective TypeScript by Dan Vanderkam Published By O'Reilly

I have truly enjoyed reading this book as of late. It is well paced and contains great detail about TypeScript and its capabilities. I feel I have gained a lot from reading it and I am only on Chapter 3 - Type Inference. I must have re-read Chapter 2 - Types, like three times and I was getting more from it each time.

Fundamentals of Software Architecture by Mark Richards and Neal Ford. Published by O'Reilly

I always find myself wondering why a decision was made in software design. Why use one technology over another? Why use this design pattern over another? Why focus on this aspect of an application opposed to another?

Fundamentals of Software Architecture has been answering a lot of those kinds of questions for me. It details what software architecture is, the responsibilities of a software architect, and goes into detail about all the required knowledge to understand software architecture fundamentals.
